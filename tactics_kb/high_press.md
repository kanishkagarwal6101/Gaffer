# High press

A **high press** is a coordinated defensive action that begins high up the pitch — typically with the forwards and attacking midfielders pressing the opponent's defenders and goalkeeper, aiming to win the ball back inside the opponent's own third. The intent is twofold: to regain possession close to the opponent's goal (a high-xG starting position), and to deny the opponent the time and space to build cleanly from the back.

A press is effective when triggers are clear (a back-pass, a heavy first touch, an underhit pass), when the pressing line is compact (small horizontal and vertical gaps so passing lanes are covered), and when the rest of the team pushes up to compress the field. Done poorly it leaves huge spaces behind the press for the opponent to exploit with one accurate long pass.

Signals in the data:
- High PPDA (passes per defensive action) for the opponent — fewer passes between pressure events means they are being harried more.
- Many ball recoveries and turnovers in the opponent's defensive third.
- Short average defensive line distance from the halfway line.
- A high share of high-xG chances created from turnovers vs from settled build-up.

Related concepts: counter-press / *gegenpressing* (pressing immediately after losing the ball), low block (the opposite shape).
