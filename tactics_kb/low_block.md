# Low block

A **low block** is a defensive shape where a team sits deep in its own half — usually with two compact lines of four in front of the goalkeeper, no more than ~30 yards of vertical space between the defensive line and the forwards — and concedes possession to the opponent on purpose. The goal is to deny space *between the lines* and in the *half-spaces*, forcing the attacking team to play in front of the block (slow, sideways) rather than through or behind it.

Teams use a low block when they are outmatched on the ball, defending a result, or playing into a counter-attacking game plan. The defensive cost is that it cedes territory and possession; the payoff is that almost every chance the opponent generates is a long-range or contested attempt with low xG.

Signals in the data:
- Average defensive line position deep in own half (low x).
- High opponent possession share but a low ratio of opponent shots inside the box.
- Many opponent passes in the middle third, few completions inside the penalty area.
- High number of clearances, blocks, and recoveries in own third.

Related concepts: mid block (line of confrontation around halfway), high press (aggressive forward pressing — the opposite of a low block).
